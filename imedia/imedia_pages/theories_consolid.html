Type Theorist Theory Description
Audience Media Effects Debate The debate as to whether media has a negative impact on the audience.
Points are normally split into 2; active and passive.
Active Audience The argument that audiences are actively involved in decoding messages in media texts.
Passive Audience The argument that audiences are unable to reject the media messages and therefore are subject to the negative effects of the media they consume.
Desensitisation The idea that the audience will become numb to the effects of a text after prolonged exposure.
Stanley Cohen Moral Panic (1972) A state of public panic as a response to a media product that is deemed threatening to certain groups or opposing moral standards within society.
Albert Bandura Social Learning (1961) Supports the passive audience theory.
The idea that children learn social behaviours through observation.
The rate of learning is accelerated when observing through TV.
Harold Laswell Hypodermic Needle (1927) Supports the passive audience theory.
Audience takes in the messages from the media without consideration.
Gerbner & Gross Cultivation Supports the passive audience theory.
The idea that repeated messages in the media alters the audience's perception of reality
The greater the repetition, the stronger the altered perception.
Stuart Hall Reception Supports the active audience theory.
The idea that the media is encoded with messages for the audience.
  • Preferred reading is when the message is fully interpreted and accepted
  • Negotiated reading is when the message is fully interpreted by the audience, but they do not fully agree with the message
  • Oppositional reading is when the message is interpreted but the audience rejects the mesesage.
Blumler & Katz Uses and Gratification Supports the active audience theory.
Audience actively seeks out media due to 4 factors:
  • Entertainment and diversion
  • Personal relationships / companionship
  • Personal identity
  • Surveillance and information
Representation David Gauntlett Pick 'n mix Supports the active audience theory.
The media is large and therefore has a large variety of representations for the audience to form their identities from.
Stuart Hall Stereotyping reduces people to a few simple characteristics / traits
These tend to belittle and represent groups as different.
Laura Mulvey Male Gaze Media is made for men, by men and therefore men are represented as masculine and assertive. And women as attractive, desirable and passive.
Liesbet Van Zoonen Feminist The idea that gender is constructed through discourse and that gender varies through culture.
For example, the idea that women are objects is a core element of western patriarchal culture.
It is also the idea that the visual codes that construct the male body as spectacle differ from those used to construct the female body.
Judith Butler Gender Performance The idea that gender is a performance(in that people use characteristics to 'perform' in a certain way) and that there are no gender identities behind gender.
Genre Rick Altman The idea that texts produce different pleasures:
  • Emotional makes the audience happy, sad or nostalgic
  • Visceral gives the audience a gut response such as excitement, fear or laughter
  • Intellectual makes the audience think
Steve Neale The idea that genres are dominated by repetition, difference, variation and change and that genres are important for marketing.
Narrative Todorov The idea that all story follows a set narrative, and therefor have a clear begining, middle, and end.
He identified 5 stages that a character should go through, these are:
  1. Equilibrium
  2. Disruption of the equilibrium
  3. Recognition of the disruption
  4. Reparation of the distruption
  5. Restoration of the equilibrium
Levi-Strauss Binary Opposites The idea that humans make sense of the world by seeing and using binary opposites
Patterns Idea that most storys have 1 major narrative, and many mini-narratives:
  • Linear
  • Non-linear
  • Multi-strand
  • Flexi-strand
  • Documentary
Devices Devices used to change the narrative. Devices such as :
  • Flashback
  • Flashforward
  • Cliffhangers
  • Red Herring
  • Breaking 4th wall
  • Chekhov's gun
  • In Media Res
Codes Codes used to identify elements of a narrative:
  • Enigma - Element of a media text that signifies mystery and keeps audience questioning
  • Action - Elements of meida texts that signify that something is going to happen as a result
  • Symbolic - Any symbol that creates a narrative, is a symbolic code
  • Semantic - Elements of a media text with a hidden meaning
  • Cultural - Elements of a media text that are only understood by a specific group of people
Exposition Exposition is basic info needed for the audience to set the narrative. This info is identified by markers.
It provides info about genre of media through conventional iconographies
Markers:
  • Geographical - Tells location
  • Temporal - Tells date and time
  • Historical - Tells the time-era
  • Generic - Tells the genre
  • Character - Tells info about the characters
Charceter Propp Idea that narratives have 8 character types:
  • The Hero
  • The Sidekick
  • The Villain
  • The False Hero
  • The Princess
  • The Princess' Father
  • The Dispatcher
  • The Donor
